Overview

EV Lacertae is a red dwarf star known for its frequent and powerful stellar flares. This project focused on analyzing those flares using light curve data, with the goal of better understanding stellar behavior and its impact on planetary habitability.

Data Collection

  • Observed through remote telescope networks
  • Captured exposure shots to detect rapid flare events
  • Analyzed brightness fluctuations using light curves

Tools & Techniques

  • AstroImageJ and DS9 for image and light curve analysis
  • Python for brightness curve smoothing and magnitude-to-flux conversions
  • Stellarium used for observing windows and planning

Scientific Goals

  • Estimate flare frequency over multiple nights
  • Relate brightness spikes to flare strength and energy
  • Assess the likelihood of planets around EV Lac supporting life

Results & Future Work

The project captured several flare events across multiple observing nights. The next step is to compare these flares to models of stellar activity and analyze their long-term impact on planetary atmospheres.
